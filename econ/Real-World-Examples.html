    Tradable Permits Eu Emissions Trading System: Implemented in 2005 Brought down emissions by 40% since 2005. Each permit allows one tonne of carbon dioxide, with 1 billion permits traded up till now.

    Pros:

    • Targets specific levels of carbon dioxide emitted
    • Incentive for innovation, to move away from carbon emitting production

    Cons:

    • Political pressures and set too high. Evident that the billions of tonnes of carbon dioxide emitted by the EU
    • Manipulation of distribution
    • Relocation
    • Needs resources to manage.
    • Volatile prices, 2008-2012 when industrial activity was low, hinders planning.

    Tax

    Carbon Tax Sweden had a tax of $150 per tonne of carbon dioxide. This decreased emissions by 30% in 1990. Economy continued to rise by 50%. Pros:

    • Specifically targets carbon emissions, instead of reducing production. Evident by the increase in economy.
    • Easier to design and implement in comparison with other market based policies.
    • Do not offer opportunities for manipulation.
    • Does not require as much monitoring

    Cons:

    • Does not target specific level
    • Regressive
    • Political pressures for the tax to be set too low to make a noticable difference. For example, exempting home heating oil from carbon tax by justin trudaeu.

    Subsidy USA: Subsidy to EV: Up to 7,500 in tax credit for producers, depending on capacity of vehicle’s battery (Non-linear supply). Lower upfront cost to produce makes it cheaper for consumers.     Recession 2008 Great Recession: Caused by subprime mortgage crisis, where loans are given with low initial interest rates that later increased significantly. This leads to many homeowners unable to meet mortgage obligations, causing a surge of foreclosures which cause downturn in housing market. Globalization of finance as a result of interbank lending made the failure of the US markets jeopardize the state of global economy as well. To escape this:

    • Decreased interest rates, tax cutes
    • Quantitative Easing: central bank purchasing financial assets to inject liquidity into financial system. restores confidence.
    • Governments intervening with massive bailout packages.

    Keynesian Fix the Great Depression US Government embraced the Keynesian approach, 1935, Franklin D Roosevelt. 13 Billion dolalrs, 250 adjusted for inflation. During the great depression, they spent large sums of money in infrastructure, job creation programs and social welfare initiatives to stimulate demand, and thus stabilizing the economy. Unemployment was as high as 25%, and it slowly dropped to 14% in 1940. Evaluation: Long-term debt
