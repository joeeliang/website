Negative Production Externality:

Tradable Permits Eu Emissions Trading System: Implemented in 2005 Brought down emissions by 40% since 2005. Each permit allows one tonne of carbon dioxide, with 1 billion permits traded up till now.

Pros:

  • Targets specific levels of carbon dioxide emitted
  • Incentive for innovation, to move away from carbon emitting production

Cons:

  • Political pressures and set too high. Evident that the billions of tonnes of carbon dioxide emitted by the EU
  • Manipulation of distribution
  • Relocation
  • Needs resources to manage.
  • Volatile prices, 2008-2012 when industrial activity was low, hinders planning.

Tax

Carbon Tax Sweden had a tax of $150 per tonne of carbon dioxide. This decreased emissions by 30% in 1990. Economy continued to rise by 50%. Pros:

  • Specifically targets carbon emissions, instead of reducing production. Evident by the increase in economy.
  • Easier to design and implement in comparison with other market based policies.
  • Do not offer opportunities for manipulation.
  • Does not require as much monitoring

Cons:

  • Does not target specific level
  • Regressive
  • Political pressures for the tax to be set too low to make a noticable difference. For example, exempting home heating oil from carbon tax by justin trudaeu.

Subsidy USA: Subsidy to EV: Up to 7,500 in tax credit for producers, depending on capacity of vehicle’s battery (Non-linear supply). Lower upfront cost to produce makes it cheaper for consumers. Increased the market share from 4% to 8%. R&D funding allowed for improved technology, increasing from 73 to 200 miles.

Subsidy Covid 19 vaccines: Positive externalities lie in the unpaid for costs of saving lives, and reduced crowding health sectors. Us government subsidized development of covid 19 vaccines. 18 Billion poured in!

Pros:

  • Immediate increase in availabillity
  • Public health improves
  • Lockdown duration decreased Cons:
  • Government funding to support provision and subsidy, opportunity costs.

Positive Externality of Consumption

Subsidy: Electric Vehicles Thailand had a subsidy for electric vehicles. 50000 Thailand Baht, 2022

Short term: Pros:

  • Environment improved
  • Development or related industries

Cons:

  • Government expenditure
    • Was so large they had to reduce it in 2023
  • Disrupts other industries
  • Equity concerns about who gets cars
  • Geographic Disparaties

Long term:

  • Price elasticity of demand is probably higher since they have more time to consider results, which makes this even more effective
  • Evident by the subsidy cut, this is unsustainable for government in the long term.
  • Market decreases dependency

Direct Government Provision: Education Local students studying in university in finland is nearly free. Pros

  • Universal access: Discrepancy between low and high income is diminished
  • Equality

Cons:

  • International students pay just as high of a fee as anywhere else in the EU: 10k a year.
  • Does not address other factors, like commute to school.
